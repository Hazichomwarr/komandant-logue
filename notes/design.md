# HM Digital Design System

This document defines the visual language of every marketing website.

The objective is to create websites that feel premium, timeless and trustworthy.

Every component should follow these principles.

---

# First Impression

The first screen should create emotion.

Visitors should immediately think:

- Professional
- Premium
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Human

Never average.

Never generic.

---

# Philosophy

Less components.

More impact.

Prefer one exceptional section over three mediocre ones.

Whitespace is part of the design.

Do not fill space just because it exists.

---

# Visual Identity

Words describing the aesthetic:

- Premium
- Editorial
- Minimal
- Elegant
- Human
- Confident
- Authentic

Avoid:

- Corporate
- Template-looking
- Cheap
- Busy
- Over-designed

---

# Layout

Maximum width

1280px

Every section should have generous vertical spacing.

Desktop

120–160px vertical padding.

Mobile

80–100px.

---

# Grid

Prefer asymmetrical layouts.

Example:

60 / 40

or

55 / 45

Avoid splitting everything 50 / 50.

It feels less dynamic.

---

# Typography

Headlines should be large.

Hero title:

56–72px

Section titles:

40–52px

Cards:

24–32px

Body:

18–20px

Small text only when necessary.

---

# Hero Sections

The hero should be emotionally strong.

Large image.

Large typography.

One sentence.

One supporting paragraph.

Maximum two CTA buttons.

Never clutter.

The hero is where trust begins.

---

# Images

Photography should feel real.

Avoid stock photos whenever possible.

Faces should look at the camera or toward the content.

Lifestyle over posed photos.

Natural lighting.

Warm colors.

High resolution.

Large images.

Never tiny images.

---

# Cards

Rounded corners.

Large padding.

Subtle borders.

Very soft shadows.

Lots of breathing room.

Cards should feel touchable.

---

# Buttons

Primary

Black

White text

Secondary

Transparent

Border

Hover:

Slight lift

Smooth transition

No flashy effects.

---

# Icons

Use icons only when they improve understanding.

Never decorate with icons.

---

# Colors

Use mostly neutral colors.

Accent colors should be intentional.

Never use more than one accent color per section.

---

# Sections

Every section must answer one question.

Hero

Who is this?

About

Why should I trust them?

Stats

Why are they credible?

Content

What do they teach?

Products

What do they recommend?

Testimonials

Who has been helped?

CTA

What should I do next?

If a section cannot answer its question, redesign it.

---

# Animations

Animations should be invisible.

People should notice the interface feels smooth.

Not that it is animated.

Preferred animations:

Fade

Fade Up

Scale

Parallax

Avoid:

Bounce

Spin

Elastic

Attention-seeking motion

---

# White Space

If something feels crowded,

remove elements before reducing spacing.

Whitespace creates luxury.

---

# Mobile

The mobile experience is equally important.

Do not simply stack desktop layouts.

Re-think spacing and hierarchy.

---

# Design References

When making design decisions, think of:

Apple

Stripe

Notion

Linear

Raycast

Airbnb

WHOOP

Nike

Vercel

Avoid inspiration from generic WordPress themes.

---

# Final Rule

Every screen should answer this question:

"Would someone believe this is a $10,000 custom website?"

If the answer is no,

continue refining.
