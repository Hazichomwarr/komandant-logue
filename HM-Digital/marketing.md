# HM Digital Marketing System

This document defines the marketing principles behind every website built by HM Digital.

A beautiful website is not enough.

Every page must help the client achieve a business goal.

---

# Philosophy

People do not buy products.

People buy outcomes.

People buy trust.

People buy stories.

People buy confidence.

Every section should move visitors one step closer to taking action.

---

# Before Designing

Understand the client's business.

Answer these questions first.

Who are they?

Who do they serve?

What problem do they solve?

Why should someone trust them?

What action should visitors take?

Never start designing before answering these questions.

---

# One Primary Goal

Every page should have ONE primary objective.

Examples:

- Book a consultation
- Purchase a product
- Join the newsletter
- Contact the business
- Schedule an appointment
- Download a guide

If multiple goals exist,

choose one primary goal and support it with secondary actions.

---

# Build Trust Before Selling

Never ask visitors to buy immediately.

Trust comes first.

Order:

Attention

↓

Interest

↓

Trust

↓

Value

↓

Action

---

# The Hero Section

The hero is not decoration.

It answers four questions within five seconds.

Who are you?

Who do you help?

Why should I trust you?

What should I do next?

A weak hero weakens the entire website.

---

# Storytelling

People remember stories more than features.

Whenever possible, tell:

The beginning.

The challenge.

The transformation.

The mission.

This applies to individuals and businesses.

---

# Benefits Over Features

Do not describe features.

Describe outcomes.

Instead of:

"Premium supplements."

Say:

"Helping you build healthier habits every day."

Instead of:

"20 years of experience."

Say:

"Helping families make healthier choices for over 20 years."

---

# Social Proof

Trust grows when other people validate the brand.

Use:

Testimonials

Reviews

Photos

Videos

Client logos

Statistics

Follower counts

Awards

Certifications

Community size

Whenever available.

---

# Calls To Action

Every section should naturally lead toward action.

Avoid generic buttons.

Prefer:

Découvrir mon univers

Voir mes recommandations

Rejoindre la communauté

Prendre rendez-vous

Commencer aujourd'hui

The CTA should continue the conversation.

---

# Content Hierarchy

Visitors scan.

They rarely read everything.

Structure content like this.

Headline

↓

Supporting sentence

↓

Visual

↓

Details

↓

CTA

Make the most important information visible first.

---

# Emotional Design

People decide emotionally.

They justify logically.

The website should create feelings of:

Trust

Hope

Confidence

Belonging

Inspiration

Avoid creating confusion.

---

# Reduce Friction

Every unnecessary click reduces conversions.

Remove anything that distracts from the primary goal.

Simplify forms.

Simplify navigation.

Simplify choices.

---

# Premium Perception

Premium brands explain less.

They show more.

Large imagery.

Clear messaging.

Generous spacing.

Confident typography.

Strong visuals.

Do not overwhelm visitors with information.

---

# Personal Branding

For creators and influencers:

The person is the brand.

People join people. They buy from people they trust.

Products support the brand.

Content builds trust.

Community creates long-term value.

Always showcase the human before the product.

---

# Business Websites

For service businesses:

Focus on the customer's problem.

Not the company's history.

The visitor should immediately understand:

How this business improves their life.

---

# E-commerce

People rarely buy from unknown stores.

Build trust before displaying products.

Explain:

Why these products?

Why this store?

Why now?

---

# Lead Generation

Whenever appropriate,

capture contact information.

Examples:

Newsletter

Free guide

Consultation

Quote request

Discovery call

Community access

Email lists become long-term business assets.

---

# Copywriting Principles

Keep sentences short.

Avoid jargon.

Write conversationally.

Use active voice.

Every paragraph should earn its place.

---

# Every Section Must Answer One Question

Hero

Who is this?

About

Why should I trust them?

Services

How can they help me?

Products

Why these products?

Testimonials

Has anyone succeeded with them?

FAQ

What objections remain?

CTA

What should I do now?

---

# Success Metric

A successful website does more than look beautiful.

Visitors should leave thinking:

"I trust this brand."

"I understand what they do."

"I know what to do next."

If any of those answers is missing,

the page is not finished.

---

# HM Digital Standard

Every project should combine:

Engineering quality.

Design excellence.

Marketing strategy.

A website succeeds when these three disciplines work together.
